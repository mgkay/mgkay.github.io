Michael G. Kay

Associate Professor

B.A. in Economics, University of Florida, 1981; M.S.I.E., 1984; Ph.D., North Carolina State University, 1992.

Areas of interest: Logistics; Genetic Algorithms; Metaheuristics; Facilities Design; Material Handling; Multisensor Integration and Fusion; Intelligent Machines and Systems.

Michael G. Kay has been a professor of Industrial Engineering at North Carolina State University since 1992. He is also an associate faculty member of the Integrated Manufacturing Systems Engineering Institute and the Operations Research Program. He has three years of engineering experience as a consultant to municipal utilities. He is a member of IEEE and IIE, and is an alumnus of CIC-MHE. 

Teaching: Facilities Design (IE 453); Material Handling Systems (IE 753); Logistics Engineering (IE 754); Real-Time Control of Automated Systems (IE 307)

Research: Public Logistics Networks; Metaheuristic Research and Applications Group at NCSU; Multimedia Sensor Fusion for Intelligent Camera Control

Software (Matlab): Matlog: Logistics Engineering Matlab Toolbox; The Genetic Algorithm Optimization Toolbox (GAOT); Basic Concepts in Matlab (PDF)
