Creating Wikipedia Content for MHI

Why Create a Wiki?

Use Wikipedia as the platform for MHI’s Industry Groups to reach the broader general public by
creating general-interest Wikipedia entries for topics related to an Industry Group’s area of focus.

  • Each wiki entry must be neutral and not reference or promote specific Membership companies
  • Direct promotion of Industry groups: Wiki entry can have External Link to Industry group’s website, and any images provided that are used on the page can cite the source (your company)
  • Indirect promotion of Membership companies through Industry group’s website
  • Wiki entry can help in setting Industry definitions and generating interest in Material Handling
  • New replacement wiki pages were created for Material Handling and Material Handling Equipment, and these pages can link to any new pages that are created.
  • New wiki page was created for CICMHE.

Basic Process to Create/Edit a Wiki

As an example of the basic process to create  a new wiki page or to make significant edits or entire rewrite to an existing page, the following is an example of ICWM, the first MHI Industry Group that did a wiki, where the group created the content for a major revision of an existing wiki page. Caster group (ICWM), lead by Ray Simmons, decided existing "Caster" wiki page was so bad that it would be best to create a new "Caster" page. Michael Kay served as editor.

  1. Develop an outline for page and assign sections to group members.
  2. Members send draft content to Michael Kay who created a new page in his personal wiki sandbox: User:Mgkay/Caster
  3. Once draft complete, a notice was posted on existing wiki page proposing a major change to the article.
  4. After waiting two weeks and receiving no comments, sandbox page replaced existing page.
  5. After registering with Wikipedia, group members can edit existing page.
  6. Group members can upload images to Wikimedia Commons:
  • When doing so, select the Creative Commons license CC BY-SA 4.0 as the permission of the file
  • Uploaded images include info about who submitted the file
  • Anyone can edit the page, and you can ask to be notified when any changes are made to the article.
  • All changes to article are recorded and you can view previous versions.
