Study Guide for Final Exam: Introduction to Operations Research¶

OR/ISE 501 - Spring 2021¶

Final Exam: 12:00-2:30p, Thu, 6 May (Individual)

This exam consists of problems that you should solve by yourself during the exam period. The exam is individual, open book, open notes, and open software. If the Instructor has determined that you have collaborated with anyone else on the exam, a violation of academic integrity will be filed.

You can solve each problem using a computer or by hand (scan to submit). The exam (a Jupyter notebook) will be emailed to you just prior to the start of the exam, and a regular class Zoom meeting will be used during the exam for you to ask any questions. The exam will consist of (a) questions related to material not covered in Exams 1 and 2 because it covered material not suitable for the take-home format of those exams; and (b) questions related to the homework and Exam 1 and 2 problems that can include (i) solving a small instance of the problem, (ii) justifying the posted solution approach, and (iii) possible extensions of the problems. The exam will not include any material related to coding in Julia and will not require the use of Julia, although you can use Julia or any other software as a calculator. Please show all your work to receive full credit. You can use the Markdown and/or code cells provided to answer each question, and you can scan and submit any handwritten answers. If Julia is used as your calculator, then the Julia code in the cells of the notebook can be used as part of your documentation of your solution.
